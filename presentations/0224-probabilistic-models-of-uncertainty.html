Example: Voting under uncertainty with a norm of unanimity
Imagine a person named "Nikita" who is a member of a 3-person committee that is taking a vote on a proposal. Nikita must choose whether to vote "yea" or "nay". Suppose the committee operates under a norm of unanimity. Specifically, if a vote is taken and some vote yea and the others vote nay, murderous violence will ensue in which some members of the committee will be killed. On the other hand if the vote is unanimous (either everyone votes "yea" or everyone votes "nay"), no one gets murdered. Model the uncertainty Nikita faces about the consequences of each vote he could cast.
Definition: Probabilistic Model of Uncertainty
A probabilistic model of uncertainty has two elements:
  • An exhaustive list of mutually exclusive possible events;
  • A probability distribution depicting the relative likelihoods with which each possible event will occur
Definition: Probability Distribution
Given an exhaustive list of mutually exclusive possible events, a probability distribution over those events is a set of numbers, consisting of exactly one number assigned to each event in the list.
  • Each number is between 0 and 1 (inclusive).
  • Together, the numbers assigned to the events sum to 1.
  • The number assigned to each event is called the "probability of" that event.
