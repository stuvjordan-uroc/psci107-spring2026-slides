Maginal and Join Probabilities -- Warm Up

Let $X$ be some conspiratorial proposition (e.g. "Sandy Hook was staged by the globalist elite as part of a conspiracy to take our guns away."), and let $Q$ be a well-known politician. Model a voter's uncertainty about two issues:

  • Is $X$ true?
  • Will $Q$ say $X$ is true?

Specifically:

  1. Build a grid to model the voter's uncertainty as joint uncertainty.
  2. Assign joint probabilities to depict the idea that the voter believes...
    • $X$ is unlikely to be true, but it's not impossible.
    • $Q$ is fairly likely to claim $X$ is true, regardless of the actual truth or falsehood of $X$.
    • $Q$ is more likely to claim $X$ is true if $X$ is actually true than if $X$ if actually false.
