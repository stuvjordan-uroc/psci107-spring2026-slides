Quick review: Numerical Spatial Model & Utility Functions
Definition: The Numerical Spatial Model
Take any set of policies differentiated by their relative degrees of something-ness.
Assign a number to each policy, with higher number assigned to policies with more something-ness!
Example: The Numerical Spatial Model
To what extent is a chicken farmer compensated per-chicken-culled when avian flu is detected in her flock?
For any given policy choice on this issue, let the number $v$ be the extent to which a farmer is compensated per-chicken culled when avian flu is detected in her flock.
Utility Representation of a Preference
Take any set of alternatives. (e.g. $A$, $B$, $C$, $D$, etc.)
A utility representation of a person's preferences over that set assigns a number $U(x)$ to each alternative $x$.
Given any pair of alternatives $x$ and $y$...
  • $U(x) > U(y)$ means "the person prefers $x$ to $y$"
  • $U(y) > U(x)$ means "the person prefers $y$ to $x$"
  • $U(x) = U(y)$ means "the person is indifferent between $x$ and $y$"
Utility Representation of a Preference: Example
My preferences over the various Harry Potter books
I despise all of them equally, and am (with one exception) completely indifferent between which I must tolerate. So...
  • $U(\text{philosopher's stone}) = 40$
  • $U(\text{chamber of secrets}) = 40$
  • $U(\text{prisoner of azkaban}) = 40$
  • $U(\text{goblet of fire}) = 41$ (I hate this slightly less than the others, because one of the children dies.)
  • $U(\text{order of the phoenix}) = 40$
  • $U(\text{half-blood prince}) = 40$
  • $U(\text{deathly hallows}) = 40$
Utility function
Take any set of alternatives. (e.g. $A$, $B$, $C$, $D$, etc.)
Suppose that every alternative has a defined value on each of a set of characteristics.
A utility function assigns a utility level to any generic alternative as a function of the that alternative's value on the characteristics.
Utility Function: Example
In the hyper-polarized politics of the U.S., all kinds of consumer products have partisan associations. Think, for instance, of music produced by the artists Bad Bunny and Kid Rock. Since the ridiculous Super Bowl kerfuffle, many persons may perceive listening to Bad Bunny as a "Democrat" thing to do and listening to Kid Rock as a "Republican" thing to do.
Let's model a person caught up in this insanity!
Imagine a person choosing which of a set of products to buy. For any given product $x$, let...
  • ...$p_x$ be a number that capture the extent to which $x$ serves its intended purpose as a product.
  • ...$r_x$ be a number that captures the percieved "Republican-ness" of purchasing $A$ and $B$.
Suppose there are two types of persons -- persons who want to "act Democrat" and persons who want to "act Republican". Let the utility of a person who wants to "act Democrat" from purchasing product $x$ be: $$ p_x -r_x $$ And let the utility of a person who wants to "act Republican" from purchasing product $x$ be: $$ p_x + r_x $$

The Tent-Shaped Utility Function

Definition: The Tent Shaped Utility Function
...models a person's preferences over policies in a numerical spatial model.
Let $x$ be the something-ness of any given policy....
Let $p$ be the person's ideal level of something-ness...
The person's utility from a policy with something-ness is $x$:
$$ - | x - p | $$
Why It's Called "Tent-Shaped"
  • In normal human language: $$ - | x - p | = \text{``the negative of the distance between x and p"} $$
  • So if $x \lt p$... $$ \text{distance between $x$ and $p$ is: } p - x $$ $$ \text{negative distance between $x$ and $p$ is: } - (p - x) = x - p $$
  • And if $x \gt p$... $$ \text{distance between $x$ and $p$ is: } x - p $$ $$ \text{negative distance between $x$ and $p$ is: } - (x - p) = p - x $$
  • So... $$ - | x - p| = \begin{cases} x - p & \text{if } x \lt p \\ p - x & \text{if } x \gt p \end{cases} $$
Why???
Because it's the simplest way to model single-peaked preferences when we need to use utility functions!
