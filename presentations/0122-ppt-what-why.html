The (general) question

The group of persons who would benefit from more housing construction in these cities overwhelming outnumbers everyone else.

(Basically, the group would would benefit = everyone who doesn't already own a home in one of those cities.)

Why don't their overwhelming numbers translate into the political power to force more housing construction?

some research...

  • In any city, adding housing anywhere requires a "special permit" from the local government.
  • Before granting a special permit, a local government always holds a public hearing, inviting any member of the public to come speak for or against granting the permit.
  • So Einstein, Glick, and Palmer looked: Who shows up to these construction permit hearings and what do that say?
