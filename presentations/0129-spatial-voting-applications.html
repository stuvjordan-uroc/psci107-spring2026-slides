Inferring ideal points from vote records

Example

Suppose we see 3 roll calls (A, B, C) in which 4 legislators (P, Q, R, S) participate. Each roll call pits two different policies against one another (So there are six distinct policies being considered!). In each roll call, one policy is identified with a vote of "Yea", and the other with a vote of "Nay". The ONLY thing we observe are the votes that each legislator casts in each roll call. Given the observed "yea"s and "nay"s, what are the possible orderings of the legislators' ideal points in a spatial model? Assume that in each roll call, each legislator votes for the alternative he finds more preferable.

You cannot solve this (and explain your work) without the Midpoint Rule: Given any pair of policies, everyone with an ideal point to the the left of the midpoint prefers the left policy to the right policy, and everyone with an ideal point to the right of the midpoint prefers the right policy to the left policy.
